Why browser extensions don't work

You've probably tried a YouTube blocker extension. Here's why it failed:

Problem 1: You can disable them in 2 seconds

Right-click the extension icon. "Remove." You're back on YouTube before your prefrontal cortex catches up. The friction is zero.

Problem 2: They only work in one browser

Blocked YouTube in Chrome? Congratulations, your brain already opened Safari. Extensions are per-browser. Your addiction is cross-browser.

Problem 3: Incognito mode bypasses them

Most extensions don't run in private browsing by default. Your brain knows this. That's why you open an incognito window.

The fix: Block at the system level

AppBlocker modifies your system's hosts file. There's no browser to switch to, no extension to disable, no incognito loophole. YouTube is blocked everywhere on your Mac.

FAQ: Blocking YouTube on Mac

Will this block YouTube in all browsers?

Yes. Chrome, Safari, Firefox, Arc, Brave, Edge, Opera — every browser. The block is at the system level (hosts file), not the browser level.

Can I block YouTube but keep YouTube Music?

Blocking youtube.com will block YouTube Music's web version too, since it runs on music.youtube.com. If you need YouTube Music, consider using the dedicated app and only blocking the youtube.com domain. Or switch to Spotify — one less Google service owning your attention.

How do I unblock YouTube when I actually need it?

Run appblocker unblock youtube.com in Terminal. It's instant. But here's the real question: do you actually need it, or do you just want it?

Does this block YouTube on other devices?

No. AppBlocker only blocks on the Mac where it's installed. If your phone is a problem too, that's a separate battle.
